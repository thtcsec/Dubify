Dubify Judge Q&A
================

Use this file as a fast-answer sheet during demo or live Q&A.

Identity
--------
Project name:
Dubify

One-line pitch:
Dubify is a Global Marketing Video Studio that combines research, storyboard review, PixVerse-powered visuals, voiceover, subtitles, and export into one production workflow.

What problem does Dubify solve?
-------------------------------
Most creators can generate isolated AI clips, but they still struggle with:
- aligning the story to the product brief
- reviewing scenes before spending credits
- producing voice and subtitles quickly
- assembling campaign-ready output

Dubify turns those scattered tools into one human-in-the-loop production pipeline.

Q1. Is this just ChatGPT + PixVerse + FFmpeg?
---------------------------------------------
Short answer:
No.

Long answer:
ChatGPT can draft text, PixVerse can generate visual clips, and FFmpeg can technically merge files.
Dubify adds the production workflow between those steps:
- brief-to-script alignment
- scene review before final render
- standardized PixVerse prompts per shot
- voiceover and subtitle automation
- final assembly and export
- provenance for PixVerse assets

That workflow layer is the product, not just the render.

Q2. Why does this belong in the PixVerse Video Generation Track?
----------------------------------------------------------------
Because PixVerse is the visual generation engine inside the workflow.
The final video uses PixVerse-generated scene assets, and Dubify builds a practical product experience around them.

This matches the guidebook requirement:
- use PixVerse to create the AI video
- provide functionality beyond playback

Dubify's beyond-playback functionality is the production pipeline itself.

Q3. Why not call PixVerse API directly?
---------------------------------------
We support a hybrid architecture because hackathon teams often receive Web membership credits before they receive or purchase Platform API credits.

For live-demo reliability, Dubify supports:
- PixVerse API-style workflow when available
- PixVerse Web/CLI asset import when that is the stable path

This avoids quota, timeout, and live-failure risk while still keeping real PixVerse-generated assets inside the pipeline.

Q4. How do you prove the shots are actually from PixVerse?
----------------------------------------------------------
Dubify stores provenance data for the final asset flow:
- sidecar JSON for PixVerse output/provenance
- output metadata in the final video
- provider label in the job status

For the external-clips demo path, the final render is marked as using PixVerse external assets rather than pretending it was API-generated.

Q5. What is your unfair advantage over simple prompt-to-video tools?
--------------------------------------------------------------------
Our unfair advantage is structured post-production workflow:
- product brief to campaign script
- scene-level human approval
- PixVerse prompt normalization
- voiceover
- subtitles
- export packaging

Simple prompt-to-video tools stop at generation.
Dubify continues into review, adaptation, and campaign-ready delivery.

Q6. Why is human-in-the-loop important here?
--------------------------------------------
Because fully automatic AI video generation often produces low-quality or misaligned output.

Dubify keeps creators in control at the highest-value checkpoint:
- script review
- scene review
- prompt editing
- asset approval before final export

That improves quality, reduces wasted credits, and keeps the workflow commercially useful.

Q7. What is the current best demo story?
----------------------------------------
The cleanest demo story is:

Product brief
-> research
-> editable script
-> scene review
-> PixVerse clips
-> voice/subtitles
-> final marketing video

Do not over-demo unrelated capabilities in one run.

Q8. Is Dubify only for research videos?
---------------------------------------
No.

The current hackathon demo path uses a marketing-video workflow because it is the strongest way to show PixVerse + product utility together.

The broader platform direction includes:
- marketing campaign production
- localization
- content repurposing

But for the hackathon, we keep the live path focused.

Q9. Where does Dubify become more valuable than CapCut?
-------------------------------------------------------
CapCut helps after you already have assets.

Dubify helps before and during production:
- turns a brief into a structured storyboard
- prepares PixVerse prompts
- organizes scene review
- automates voice + subtitle + final render

So the value is not only editing.
It is workflow orchestration.

Q10. Why is this useful for marketing teams?
--------------------------------------------
Marketing teams need speed and consistency.

Dubify lets them go from one product brief to a campaign-ready story faster:
- fewer manual handoffs
- faster scene approval
- faster voice/subtitle packaging
- cleaner export workflow

That is especially useful for creator-led launches and social-first campaigns.

Q11. What happens after the hackathon?
--------------------------------------
The strongest next steps are:
- multi-language campaign variants
- content repurposing into multiple formats
- deeper clip editing / asset replacement
- stronger motion system beyond still-scene fallback

The hackathon build proves the core workflow and PixVerse integration path.

Q12. If I am a judge, what should I remember in one sentence?
-------------------------------------------------------------
PixVerse gives Dubify the visual shots, and Dubify turns them into a reviewable, voiced, subtitled, campaign-ready production workflow.
